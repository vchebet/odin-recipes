Pan-Seared Scallops

A classic pan-seared scallop recipe involves patting dry large sea scallops, seasoning them with salt and pepper, and searing them in a hot skillet with butter and oil for 2–3 minutes per side until a golden crust forms and the center is just opaque. Serve immediately with a squeeze of fresh lemon and a garnish of chopped parsley for a simple yet elegant dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large sea scallops
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley (optional)

    • Steps

    1. Pat the scallops dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture. This helps achieve a good sear.
    2. Season the scallops with salt and freshly ground black pepper on both sides.
    3. Heat the butter and olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until the butter is melted and the mixture is hot but not smoking.
    4. Add the scallops to the skillet in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd them. You may need to cook them in batches.
    5. Sear the scallops for 2–3 minutes on each side, or until they develop a golden-brown crust and are just opaque in the center. Avoid overcooking, as scallops can become tough.
    6. Remove the scallops from the skillet and transfer them to a serving plate. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the top and garnish with chopped parsley if desired.
    7. Serve immediately while hot, perhaps alongside a light salad or steamed vegetables for a complete meal.
